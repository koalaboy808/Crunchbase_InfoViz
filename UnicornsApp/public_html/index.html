A startup is a new business venture in the form of a company or organization designed to search for a repeatable and scalable business model. These companies are innovative in a process of development, validation and research for target markets. Funds to start these businesses are provided by investors such as venture capitalists through various rounds. TechCrunch has created a platform, called Crunchbase, which is basically a Wikipedia for startups and investors. With the help of this crowd sourced data, our visualization aims to extract information about funding by venture capitalists, and the progress of startups through their stages of funding.

WHAT MARKETS ARE UNICORNS GETTING FUNDING FOR?
Unicorns, companies valued at over $1 Billion dollars, attract slightly different types of funding than the entire startup market. This data shows that although BioTech is highly funded in the startup world, many unicorns are not in that market. Unicorns in E-Commerce, Advertising, and Software are the most highly funded. Click on the different markets to see how they compare to the entire startup market as well as to see how individual startups were funded over time.
